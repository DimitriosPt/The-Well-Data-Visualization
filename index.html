Dataset

The Dataset I've explored is a collection of attendance records from CSUS's very own gym, The Well. The Well first opened in 2010 and has since been a popular addition to the campus.

The dataset highlights a variety of important attendance information. Data is seperated by gender, days of the week, and the grand totals for entire years. I have additionally added data to observe trends against weather and to model attendance against the school population. The majority of the data I used was provided to me by The Well.
As an avid user of The Well facility, my primary goal in exploring this dataset was finding ways to optimize my workouts- primarily by finding when the gym was the least populated. This drove me to explore a variety of avenues such as weather and day of the week to determine when the best time for me to go to the gym was to avoid waiting on other people.

Discoveries & Insights

Hottest Times To Go To The Gym.

The first thing I wanted to observe was the best times to go to the gym. When I had first envisioned exploring this question I was hoping The Well would also have timestamps for when people left so I could map overall population in the gym, but that data is not recorded unfortunately. The most interesting thing about the trends shown here is that it is bimodal whereas I expected a mostly normal distribution around 1:30 or so.
